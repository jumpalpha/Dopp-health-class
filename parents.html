About the Course
What is the Legend of Wellness?

The Legend of Wellness is a semester-long high school health course built around a quest-based learning model. Students work through 28 chapters of updated health content organized into 9 themed "realms," completing activities, vocabulary games, and culminating "boss battle" assessments at the end of each unit.

The course content is drawn from the Glencoe Health textbook, fully modernized with the most current medical guidelines, statistics, and health recommendations — including content that didn't exist when older textbooks were written, such as the fentanyl crisis, COVID-19, updated HPV vaccine information, and revised cardiovascular disease guidelines.

The website at jumpalpha.github.io/Dopp-health-class is the central hub. Everything students need — chapter readings, vocabulary practice, interactive games, and their personal progress tracker — is there, accessible from any device with a browser.

Course Content
What Will My Student Learn?

The course covers all 7 dimensions of wellness — physical, mental, emotional, social, intellectual, environmental, and occupational. Click any topic below to learn more about what's taught and why it matters.

🧠Mental & Emotional HealthRealm II▶
Students learn to identify and express emotions healthily, build self-esteem and resilience, recognize signs of mental health conditions in themselves and others, and understand how to seek help. Suicide prevention and eating disorders are covered with age-appropriate, evidence-based content. We address the stigma around mental health directly — normalizing help-seeking behavior is a key goal of this unit.
Note: If your student wants to discuss anything from this unit at home, we encourage those conversations.
💞Relationships, Conflict & Violence PreventionRealm III▶
This realm covers what makes relationships healthy, how to recognize and respond to conflict constructively, peer pressure and refusal skills, bullying and cyberbullying (including how to be an upstander), and dating violence warning signs. Students practice real communication skills through role-play activities. Family relationships — including how to navigate difficult family situations — are also covered.
💀Substances — Medicines, Tobacco, Alcohol & DrugsRealm VII▶
This is one of the most critical units in the course. Students learn the science behind addiction, the current state of the opioid crisis (including fentanyl in counterfeit pills — the #1 teen drug death risk today), the real risks of alcohol on the developing brain, and why teen tobacco and vaping rates have dropped to 25-year lows. Students also learn about naloxone (Narcan) — an OTC overdose reversal medication that could save a life. The goal is honest, factual information that equips teens to make informed decisions — not scare tactics.
Important: If you have concerns about your student's substance use, please contact Mr. Dopp or your school counselor.
🏥Communicable Diseases, STIs & HIV/AIDSRealm VIII▶
Students learn how diseases spread, how the immune system fights them, and how vaccines work (including mRNA vaccines). The STI unit covers the most common infections, prevention methods, and updated treatment information — including the current HPV vaccine (Gardasil 9) and the U=U principle for HIV. Abstinence is taught as the only 100% effective prevention method, alongside accurate information about other prevention strategies. This content is taught factually and without judgment.
💓First Aid & Emergency PreparednessRealm IX▶
Students learn updated CPR guidelines (including Hands-Only CPR — no rescue breaths required for untrained bystanders), how to use an AED, how to respond to choking, shock, and poisoning, and how to build an emergency preparedness kit. This is practical life-saving knowledge. Students may practice compression technique in class.
At Home
How Can I Support My Student?

Health education is most effective when it continues at home. Here are concrete ways to reinforce what students are learning:

💬
Ask What They Learned
"What realm are you on?" is a great conversation starter. Students who can explain a concept have truly learned it. Ask them to teach you something from their current chapter.
🎮
Play the Games Together
All 15 games are open to anyone. Pull up Myth Busters or Would You Rather at home — you might be surprised what you learn alongside your student.
🧪
Try the Habit Tracker
In Realm I, students choose a health habit to build over 2 weeks. Ask about their habit — even better, try it alongside them for accountability and bonding.
📱
Know the Fentanyl Facts
The single most important safety message: any pill not from a licensed pharmacy can be lethal. Please reinforce this at home. It applies to anything offered at a party, school, or through social media.
🔒 A Note on Privacy & Content

The Legend of Wellness website is public — any parent or guardian can view everything at jumpalpha.github.io/Dopp-health-class. There are no login-gated areas. All chapter PDFs, games, and activities are openly accessible.

The student progress tracker (quest.html) saves data locally in the student's own browser — it is not sent to any server and is not visible to the teacher or other students without the student sharing it. The class leaderboard (leaderboard.html) is an opt-in feature where students choose whether to post their score.

If you have concerns about any specific content in the course, please reach out directly to Mr. Dopp. We welcome that conversation.

Frequently Asked Questions
Parents Ask Us
Does my student need to create an account or give personal information? +
No. The entire site works without any account, login, or personal information. The quest tracker asks for a name and avatar choice, but that's stored only in the browser on that device — it's never transmitted anywhere.
Can my student access the site from home? +
Yes — the site works on any device with a browser: phone, tablet, laptop, Chromebook. The URL is jumpalpha.github.io/Dopp-health-class. All games and chapter content are available with no special software.
Why is health education taught as a "game"? +
Research consistently shows that students retain information better when it's presented in engaging, low-stakes formats. The game structure — quests, boss battles, badges — creates motivation without reducing the seriousness of the content. Every game question comes directly from the textbook. The Health Jeopardy boss battle covers the same material as a traditional test, but with higher engagement and lower anxiety.
Is the content age-appropriate? +
Yes. All content is written for high school students and aligns with standard health education curriculum. Topics like STIs and substance abuse are covered factually and without graphic content — with the same approach as any accredited high school health course. If you have specific concerns about any topic, please contact Mr. Dopp directly.
How is the course graded? +
Grading is determined by Mr. Dopp and follows school policy. The website's quest tracker and leaderboard are supplemental tools — they're designed to motivate students, not to replace formal assessment. Check your school's grading system or contact Mr. Dopp for specifics on how assignments and boss battles factor into grades.
My student seems uncomfortable with something they learned. What should I do? +
That discomfort is often a sign that important content landed. Health education is meant to be informative, sometimes even uncomfortable — that's how it changes behavior. If your student has questions or seems distressed, we encourage an open conversation. You can also contact Mr. Dopp directly, and your school counselor is always available as a resource.
I noticed something on the site I have concerns about. Who do I contact? +
Please reach out directly to Mr. Dopp through your school's standard communication channels. The website is built and maintained by the teacher and is updated regularly — feedback from parents is genuinely welcome and helps improve the course.
